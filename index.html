Facts you probably did not know about Germany !
Escaping from prison is not illegal

In Germany, attempting to break out of jail is not punishable by law. It’s considered a basic human instinct to seek freedom, as long as no other crimes (like hurting someone) are committed during the escape.

Fanta was invented in Germany

During WWII when Coca-Cola syrup couldn’t be imported due to the war, the German branch of the company created a new drink using available ingredients and that’s how Fanta was born.

Baby name and The Government

German authorities (the Standesamt) can reject a baby name if they believe it could harm the child’s well-being or is inappropriate. There are strict rules around this.

Beer is legally considered a food

In Germany, beer counts as a staple food item rather than just an alcoholic drink. This cultural view makes it perfectly normal to enjoy beer with lunch or dinner and in some places, it has even been cheaper than bottled water. Germany’s beer culture is incredibly deep and diverse over 1,300–1,500 breweries and thousands of beer varieties!

Zoos'

With over 400 zoos, Germany leads the world in this category, a reflection of its strong emphasis on wildlife, education, and conservation.

A1 Level

In this foundational level, you will explore all the nuances, rules, and "ifs and buts" of the German language. Your journey begins at the very absolute roots of mastering the alphabet, phonics, and numbers before rapidly expanding into essential vocabulary and daily life conversations. From ordering your morning coffee to introducing your family, you will dive into the core grammar mechanics that hold the language together.

A2 Level

In this level, you will step beyond basic survival phrases and unlock the true depth of daily conversation. This stage is all about building confidence, expanding your vocabulary, and mastering the fluid sentence structures that make you sound like a natural speaker. You will learn to share your past experiences, debate everyday topics, express personal opinions, and navigate complex real-world situations like doctor visits, work environments, and travel mishaps. By deep-diving into essential past tenses, relative clauses, and missing case structures, Level 2 bridges the gap between structured textbook learning and spontaneous, real-life expression.

B1 Level

At this level, the focus shifts entirely from routine conversations to true linguistic independence. B1 is the threshold where a learner stops just reacting to German and starts genuinely living in it, allowing them to express abstract thoughts, argue a point, and navigate professional or academic settings without constant guidance. In this level empowers you to navigate everyday professional environments, read authentic media articles, and feel entirely at home in any German speaking country.
